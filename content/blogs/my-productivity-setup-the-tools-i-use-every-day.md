Hello everyone!

Today's blog post is about my productivity setup and the tools I use to stay organized and productive in my daily life. Whether it's managing tasks, taking notes, tracking projects, or simply organizing ideas, having the right tools can make a huge difference.

Over the years, I've tried countless productivity apps and systems. I've switched between different to-do lists, note-taking platforms, project managers, and trackers to find what actually works for me. After a lot of experimentation, I've settled on a simple setup that helps me stay organized without adding unnecessary complexity.

Here are the tools I'm currently using and why I prefer them.

## Google Tasks – My Everyday To-Do List

There are hundreds of to-do list apps available today, but the only one I truly need is **Google Tasks**.

I'm a big fan of Google's ecosystem because everything just works. There's no complicated setup process it's available on your phone, laptop, tablet, or any device where you can sign in with your Google account. Your tasks sync automatically, it's completely free, and it's incredibly lightweight.

Unlike many other productivity apps, Google Tasks focuses on the essentials. It allows me to create lists, organize tasks, add due dates, and get things done without overwhelming me with unnecessary features.

Sometimes, simplicity is the best productivity tool.

## Google Keep – Fast and Simple Note Taking

Another application I use daily is **Google Keep**.

Many people think it lacks advanced features compared to other note-taking apps, but that's exactly why I like it. Most of the time, I don't need complicated formatting or endless customization options. I just need a blank space to quickly capture an idea, create a checklist, or save important information.

One feature I particularly love is its Audio recording capability. In my experience, the speech recognition is fast and accurate, making it perfect for capturing thoughts while on the move.

It's clean, simple, and does exactly what I need from a note-taking app.

## AFFiNE – My Alternative to Notion

The next tool on my list is **AFFiNE**, which many people might not know about.

For me, it's one of the best alternatives to Notion. It provides almost everything that I need for project management and knowledge organization while being faster and more responsive.

AFFiNE includes calendar views, database tables, Kanban boards, note-taking capabilities, and one of my favorite features—journaling. The editor supports bullet points, shortcuts, formatting options, and all the text-editing tools I need for daily work.

Another major advantage is its offline mode. Even without an internet connection, I can continue working seamlessly. AFFiNE Cloud also provides a generous amount of free storage, which is more than enough for my personal projects and notes.

The mobile experience is what truly makes it stand out for me. Notion is an incredibly powerful tool with a huge ecosystem, but in my personal experience, its mobile app feels slower, especially when syncing larger workspaces between devices. When you're trying to stay productive, waiting for applications to load can become frustrating.

AFFiNE feels much smoother and more responsive, which makes it a better fit for my workflow.

## Google Workspace vs Microsoft Office

I generally prefer using **Google Workspace** over Microsoft Office because of its excellent cloud synchronization and accessibility.

Everything is stored in Google Drive, which means I can access my files from any device at any time. The online collaboration and real-time syncing are some of the biggest reasons why I continue to use Google's ecosystem.

That being said, I still use **Microsoft Word and Excel**, especially when working offline or when I need advanced features that Google's productivity tools don't provide.

One issue I've frequently encountered is the compatibility between the two platforms. When moving documents from Microsoft Word to Google Docs, there are often small adjustments that need to be made. Font sizes, line spacing, margins, headers, footers, and even page layouts can sometimes change, which becomes noticeable when printing or editing professional documents.

It's not a major problem, but it does require a few workarounds whenever I switch between the two ecosystems.

For my day-to-day work, however, Google Workspace remains my preferred choice because of its simplicity and seamless synchronization.

## The Built-In Clock App

One of the most underrated productivity tools I use every day is the built-in clock application on my devices.

I use it for alarms, timers, and Pomodoro sessions. Since these features are already included natively, I don't really see the need to install additional timer or alarm applications.

Sometimes, the best tools are the ones that already exist on your device.

## Final Thoughts

At the end of the day, productivity isn't about using the most powerful or feature-packed applications. It's about finding tools that fit your workflow and help you get things done with the least amount of friction.

For me, that means choosing simplicity, fast synchronization, and reliability over endless customization. My current setup Google Tasks, Google Keep, AFFiNE, Google Workspace, and the native clock app gives me everything I need to stay organized and focused without overcomplicating things.

Of course, everyone's workflow is different, but if these tools align with your needs, they're definitely worth trying.
