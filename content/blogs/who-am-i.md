# **Hi everyone,**

Welcome to my blog.

You might be wondering, *why start a blog in 2026?* With so much information already available online, it can feel like everything has already been said. There are thousands of blogs, videos, podcasts, and social media posts covering almost every topic imaginable.

So why am I doing this?

For me, this blog isn't about becoming famous, gaining followers, or building a personal brand. It's about having a space where I can express myself, put my thoughts into words, and document my journey.

I want to create something that my future self can look back on years from now. A place where I can remember what I was learning, struggling with, building, and dreaming about at this stage of my life.

If my experiences can help someone avoid a mistake, learn a new skill, gain confidence, or simply realize they're not alone in their struggles, then this blog will have served its purpose.

## **A Little About Me**

My name is Ashish Alva, and I'm from Karnataka, India.

I recently completed my Bachelor's degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from a Tier-2 engineering college. Like many graduates, I am currently in the transition phase between college and professional life.

I have received a job offer and am currently waiting for my official offer letter. While I wait for the next chapter of my career to begin, I decided this was the perfect time to start documenting my thoughts, experiences, and lessons.

But this blog isn't just about engineering or technology.

It's about the journey of figuring out who I am and what I want to become.

## **Growing Up with Expectations**

In India, most of us grow up hearing the same advice.

Become a doctor.

Become an engineer.

Get a stable white-collar job.

Build a secure future.

It's a path that millions of people follow. Some call it a rat race. Others call it chasing a carrot that's always hanging just out of reach.

But for me, it was never about blindly following the crowd.

What always interested me was the actual meaning behind engineering.

Engineering is not just about getting placed in a company or collecting a salary every month. At its core, engineering is about solving problems, building useful things, creating value, and making life better for people.

Unfortunately, many students are limited by the opportunities available to them, the resources they have access to, and the support they receive. Sometimes we know what we want to achieve, but we don't always have the environment or guidance needed to get there.

## **Trying Different Paths**

Because of that, I've spent a large part of my life exploring different interests and trying to discover where I truly belong.

I've explored art. Who doesn’t love drawing, painting right? I tried it and as it turned out i was pretty bad at it so what did i give up NO i still do draw and that too still pretty bad but who cares, i want to do something and i do it.

I bought a laptop and started experimenting with everything I could find. I tried UI/UX design, graphic design, digital art, drawing, content creation, and many other creative pursuits.

I was fascinated by the idea of creating things from scratch and bringing ideas to life.

I've also invested a lot of time in fitness.

From calisthenics to CrossFit and regular workouts, fitness has always been an important part of my life. For me, working out has never been about building the biggest physique possible. It's about staying healthy, building discipline, improving my mindset, and becoming stronger both physically and mentally.

But as life often reminds us, things don't always go according to plan.

Some interests stayed with me.

Some didn't.

Some dreams changed.

Others evolved.

And that's completely okay.

## **What I Learned**

Every skill I explored taught me something.

Every success gave me confidence.

Every failure gave me perspective.(still hurts though - but you move on)

The things that didn't work out were often just as valuable as the things that did.

Over time, I realized that life isn't always about finding the perfect path immediately. Sometimes it's about exploring different roads until you discover what genuinely excites you.

Today, technology and problem-solving are the areas that continue to pull me back.

I enjoy building things.

I enjoy learning.

I enjoy finding solutions to problems.

And most importantly, I enjoy creating things that can be useful to others.

## **Why This Blog Exists**

This blog is my attempt to document that journey.

I'll be writing about technology, engineering, career growth, personal development, productivity, fitness, lessons from failures, and everything else that helps me grow as a person.

Some posts may be technical.

Some may be personal.

Some may simply be reflections on experiences I've had.

But every post will be honest.

This blog is not about pretending to have all the answers.

It's about learning publicly, sharing experiences, and continuously improving.

If you're a student trying to figure out your future, someone struggling to find direction, or simply a curious person who enjoys learning, I hope you'll find something valuable here.

Thank you for taking the time to read this.

This is just the beginning.

Whoami 

Ashish Alva

Trying to figure out life
